FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Hike Analytics different from other hiking apps?

We're the only hiking app built on peer-reviewed research. Every metric—Peak-30 cadence, ACWR, Cost of Transport—comes from published studies. We cite 50+ scientific papers and explain the evidence behind each recommendation. It's not step counting; it's biomechanics and exercise physiology applied to hiking.

What is Peak-30 cadence and why does it matter?

Peak-30 cadence is your average steps per minute during the best 30 consecutive minutes of hiking each day. A breakthrough study of 78,500 people (Del Pozo-Cruz, JAMA 2022) showed it independently predicts mortality risk—even after controlling for total daily steps. We track this automatically, something no other app does.

How does ACWR prevent injuries?

Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio compares your recent training (last 7 days) to your longer-term average (28 days). Research shows ACWR >1.50 increases injury risk 2-4 times. We calculate this daily and warn you before dangerous spikes, helping you progress safely with 5-10% weekly increases.

Why cadence zones instead of heart rate zones?

Cadence is more reliable and practical. The CADENCE-Adults study proved 100 spm = moderate intensity (3 METs) with 86% sensitivity and 90% specificity. Heart rate varies with heat, stress, illness, caffeine—cadence doesn't. Plus, you don't need a chest strap or watch; just count steps.

Can Hike Analytics help with fall prevention?

Yes. We track clinical fall risk indicators: gait speed <0.8 m/s, double support>35%, asymmetry (GSI) >10%, and hiking steadiness. These are evidence-based thresholds from geriatric research. Early detection allows intervention before falls occur—critical for adults 65+.

How does Hike Analytics protect my privacy?

All data stays on your iPhone—period. No cloud sync, no accounts, no servers receiving your health data. We process everything locally using on-device algorithms. Your hiking metrics, location, heart rate—completely private. We follow GDPR and HIPAA philosophy even though we're not legally required to (since we never see your data).

Do I need special equipment?

No. Hike Analytics works with iPhone or Apple Watch. For basic metrics (cadence, distance, steps), just your phone. For heart rate data and more accurate metrics, an Apple Watch helps but isn't required. We integrate with Apple Health, so any compatible device works.

Is Hike Analytics suitable for rehabilitation?

Absolutely. We're used by rehab patients tracking recovery objectively. The Gait Symmetry Index (GSI) formula quantifies left-right differences (<3% normal,>10% clinically significant). Monitor stride length recovery, hiking speed improvement, and asymmetry reduction. Share objective data with your physical therapist or physician.

What's the scientific basis for the 11 formulas?

Each formula includes validation data and original research citation. Example: Moore's cadence→METs equation (R²=0.87, ±0.5 METs accuracy, 76 adults) is 23-35% more accurate than older ACSM equations. We show the science, not just black-box calculations.
