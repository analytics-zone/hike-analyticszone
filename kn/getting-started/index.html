Getting Started with Hike Analytics

Complete guide to setting up Hike Analytics and understanding your hiking analytics journey

Quick Start Guide

Welcome to Hike Analytics! This guide will help you get started with advanced gait analysis and hiking performance tracking in just a few minutes.

Step 1: Download and Install

  1. Download Hike Analytics from the App Store
  2. Open the app on your iPhone or iPad
  3. Grant permission to access Apple Health when prompted
  4. Start your 7-day free trial (no credit card required)

Step 2: Connect Apple Health

Hike Analytics analyzes your hiking workouts from Apple Health:

  • Automatic Import: Your existing hiking workouts are imported automatically
  • Real-time Sync: New hikes from Apple Watch or iPhone are analyzed instantly
  • Privacy First: All data stays on your device - no cloud upload

Step 3: Understand Your Dashboard

The main dashboard shows your key hiking metrics:

  • Recent Hikes: Your latest hiking workouts with key stats
  • Weekly Summary: Total distance, time, and Hiking Stress Score (WSS)
  • Gait Metrics: Stride symmetry, cadence, and efficiency scores
  • Training Zones: Time spent in each heart rate zone

Key Concepts

Gait Analysis

Gait analysis examines how you hike. Hike Analytics tracks:

  • Stride Length: Distance covered per stride
  • Cadence: Steps per minute
  • Stride Symmetry: Balance between left and right legs
  • Ground Contact Time: How long your foot touches the ground
  • Double Support Time: When both feet are on the ground simultaneously

Learn more about gait analysis fundamentals.

Hiking Zones

Training in different intensity zones provides specific health benefits:

  • Zone 1 (Recovery): Very light hiking for recovery
  • Zone 2 (Fat Burn): Optimal for fat burning and aerobic base
  • Zone 3 (Aerobic): Cardiovascular fitness improvement
  • Zone 4 (Threshold): High-intensity aerobic capacity
  • Zone 5 (Peak): Maximum effort for short bursts

Explore the complete hiking zones guide.

Hiking Stress Score (WSS)

WSS quantifies the physiological stress of each hike:

  • Based on heart rate intensity and duration
  • Helps balance training load with recovery
  • Prevents overtraining and optimizes progress
  • Track weekly and monthly trends

Calculate your WSS with our WSS calculator.

Recording Your First Hike

Using Apple Watch

  1. Start a "Hiking" workout on your Apple Watch
  2. Hike naturally - no need to change your form
  3. End the workout when finished
  4. Hike Analytics automatically imports and analyzes it

Using iPhone Only

  1. Enable "Fitness Tracking" in iPhone Settings
  2. Carry your iPhone during hikes
  3. Hiking activities are logged automatically
  4. Review in Hike Analytics for analysis

Pro Tips for Success

1. Start with Baseline Assessment

Record 2-3 normal hikes first to establish your baseline metrics before making changes.

2. Focus on Zone 2 Hiking

Spend 60-80% of your hiking time in Zone 2 for optimal health benefits and fat burning.

3. Monitor Gait Symmetry

If symmetry drops below 90%, consider form work or consulting a professional to prevent injury.

4. Build Gradually

Increase weekly WSS by no more than 10% per week to avoid overtraining.

5. Consistency Over Intensity

Regular moderate hiking beats occasional intense hikes for long-term health.

Troubleshooting

Metrics Not Showing

  • Ensure Apple Health permissions are granted
  • Check that workouts are properly labeled as "Hiking" in Health app
  • For heart rate zones, verify heart rate data is being recorded

Inaccurate Stride Metrics

  • Calibrate Apple Watch by hiking outdoor routes with accurate GPS
  • Ensure proper watch fit (snug but comfortable)
  • Hike with natural arm swing for better motion detection
